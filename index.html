Research on understanding caring needs

Introduction

We are looking for people who care for their loved ones (mom, dad, aunt, spouse etc.) to take part in a research study. The focus of this study will be on your caring habits. We aim to use these findings to understand how to respond and manage the needs of the people who care for their loved ones in a more efficient and effective way.

If you participate you will be invited for an interview (Phase 1) to get a sense of your caring process (the interview could be over Skype or face-to-face depending on your preferences). After the interview, you will be asked to participate in a diary study (Phase 2) for a week which should take a maximum of 10 minutes each day. At the end of the study you will be invited to take part in a Focus group (Phase 3) in London where you will be provided a chance to communicate with other participants in this study to exchange views together.

All the phases mentioned above would be organized at your convenience. It will be up to you to decide to take part in either of the phases. During this study, you will not be asked to provide any access to the people you care for.

About this study

This study is being conducted by researchers from University College London, UK. The study has been approved by the UCLIC Research Department’s Ethics Chair [Project ID No]: UCLIC/1415/011/MSc Berthouze/Das/Anand.

Please note that by completing this form you are giving us permission to contact you in relation to this study. If you are chosen to participate in the study we will contact you via email to arrange a date and time for the interview at your convenience. Information gained in interviews will be used for the purpose of academic research and may be presented in an academic publication and/or conferences, workshops and/or teaching material. Confidentiality and anonymity will be maintained, and it will not be possible to identify you from any such publications. During the interview, an audio recording will be made for later transcription. Your details will NOT be used for any purposes other than this. You are free to withdraw from the study at any time.

Note: All data will be collected and stored in accordance with the Data Protection Act 1998 and will be kept anonymous.

If you have any questions or encounter any problems you may contact us at arindra.das.14@ucl.ac.uk

Requirements
In order to participate you must be:

  • Caring for your loved ones.
  • The person you are caring for is over the age of 60.
  • You are aged 18 or over.
If you fulfil these requirements and wish to participate in our study, we would love to hear from you so please complete your details in the form below.

Why this study?

The growth of an ageing population and people with long-term conditions is a challenge to the current health care strategy. King fund organization’s [Appleby 2013] published report points out, the people with long-term conditions, i.e., around 15 million, will rise by a third in the next 10 years. Users with long-term conditions will account for more than 70% of inpatient beds and half of all general practitioners (GP) appointments in England [Wilson et al., 2013].
