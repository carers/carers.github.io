Understanding the impact of technology interventions on people who care for their loved ones

We are doing a study that aims to gain a better understanding of how technology can be used by individuals caring for their loved ones during caring sessions. We aim to use these findings to develop better technology that can respond and manage the needs of the individuals in a more efficient and effective way. We would like to invite you to participate in this research project.

Why act now?

The growth of an ageing population and people with long-term conditions is a challenge to the current health care strategy. King fund organization’s [Appleby 2013] published report points out, the people with long-term conditions, i.e., around 15 million, will rise by a third in the next 10 years. Users with long-term conditions will account for more than 70% of inpatient beds and half of all general practitioners (GP) appointments in England [Wilson et al., 2013].
